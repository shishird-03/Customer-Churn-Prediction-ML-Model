Key Insights

Total Charges and Tenure are consistently the strongest predictors — long-standing customers with higher cumulative spend are less likely to churn. Contract type also plays a major role: month-to-month customers churn at significantly higher rates than those on annual or biennial plans. Fiber optic customers show elevated churn, likely due to pricing sensitivity.

Training Details
How the model was built, preprocessed, and optimized.
Dataset

IBM Telco Customer Churn Dataset — 7,043 customer records, 21 raw features ({{ total_features }} after one-hot encoding). Source: Kaggle / IBM Cloud Pak for Data.

Train / Test Split

80/20 stratified split, ensuring proportional churn representation in both the training and evaluation sets.

Class Balancing — SMOTE

SMOTE (Synthetic Minority Oversampling) was applied only to training data to address the class imbalance (~26% churn rate), preventing the model from ignoring minority class patterns.

Hyperparameter Tuning

GridSearchCV with 3-fold cross-validation was used to find the optimal combination of n_estimators, max_depth, min_samples_split, and min_samples_leaf.

Preprocessing Pipeline

  1. Converted TotalCharges from string to numeric; filled 11 NaN values with median.
  2. Label encoded binary columns (Partner, Dependents, PhoneService, PaperlessBilling, Churn, gender).
  3. One-hot encoded multi-class columns (Contract, InternetService, PaymentMethod, and service add-ons) with drop_first=True.
  4. Removed outliers using Z-score threshold > 3.
  5. Applied SMOTE on training split only.
